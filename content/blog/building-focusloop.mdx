## The Problem

I was spending 4–5 hours a day on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts. Most of it was mindless — the kind of scrolling where you open the app with no intent and close it 45 minutes later wondering where the time went.

I'm not alone. The average Gen-Z spends **6.5 hours on screens daily**, most of it passive consumption.

## The Idea

FocusLoop is simple: a 30-day challenge app that replaces your doomscrolling habit with intentional tasks. Each day has 3 tasks. Complete all 3, streak continues. Fail, and you start over.

The psychology is borrowed from habit research — specifically the idea that **replacing a habit** is far more effective than trying to simply eliminate it.

## The Stack

I kept it as lean as possible:

- **React + Vite** — no server needed
- **Tailwind CSS** — rapid UI iteration
- **localStorage** — no backend, no auth, no friction

The entire app is a SPA. Data lives in your browser. Nothing is tracked.

## What I Learned

**1. Constraints force creativity.** No backend meant I had to get creative with data structure. Everything is keyed by user state in localStorage.

**2. Dark mode first.** The target user is on their phone at night. A dark, high-contrast UI just makes sense.

**3. Micro-animations matter.** Adding Framer Motion to task completions and streak milestones made the app feel *alive*. Users noticed.

## What Didn't Work

The first version had a complicated onboarding flow. Three screens, a survey, a "commitment" modal. Users bounced. I stripped it to one screen — your challenge starts now.

## The Result

FocusLoop is live. It's not a business. It's a tool I built for myself and shipped because other people might need it too.

That's enough reason.
